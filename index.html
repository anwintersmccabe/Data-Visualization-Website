Coronavirus in the United States

by Annabel Winters-McCabe

This site uses interactive visualizations to explore the spread of COVID-19 throughout the United States, and particularly the effects of the delayed onset of widespread testing. This project is inspired by the constant Coronavirus coverage on the news, and the emphasis on lack of testing in the U.S. specifically. The difficulty of some states to acquire enough COVID-19 tests has been widely publicized, and through these visualizations I hope to understand the relationship between coronavirus testing, hospitalizations, and deaths from coronavirus. All data comes from the COVID Tracking project. I used this dataset because it is updated daily and includes information about daily increases as well as cumulative totals. Limitations of this set include not accounting for coronavirus related state-wide orders and other factors that could be effecting these numbers, and also some of the data categories lack explanation or are incomplete. This dataset also does not include population information for states, which would be helpful to have to put the numbers in perspective.
